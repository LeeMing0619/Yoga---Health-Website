Sound Bath/Gong Bath

What is a sound bath?

Sound bath is the easiest form of meditation, where all you need to do is lay down and let the healing sounds of the alchemy bowls wash over you. A sound bath is a relaxation technique and meditative experience whereby participants ‘bathe’ in the sound waves produced by instruments such as crystal singing bowls, chimes, gongs, drums. Sound Therapy has been used for thousands of years in many different cultures all with the same intention; to move us from a place of imbalance to balance.

What happens during a sound bath?

When you get in the studio you will lay down on the mat with you head facing the crystal singing bowls, you can cover yourself with a blanket to get cozy as some people can get cold, use an eye cover and just relax. You will be guided through light breathing exercises, body scan and relaxation meditation and then all you need to do is to just relax to the sounds of the crystal singing bowls or gongs. Everything in the whole universe is vibrating all the time – even things that appear still, and even the cells of our bodies. As we are made up of up to 80% water, our bodies are brilliant conductors of sound, in the session your brain waves change from a Beta (waking state) to Theta & Delta (deep meditation state), this puts the body in the best state to heal itself, unlock blockages, release traumas and bring you to a deep relaxation.

What happens after the soundbath?

At the end of your sound bath session, the practitioner will slowly bring you back to reality with some focused breathing techniques, and you will feel a lot lighter and calmer than before, a new sense of clarity, like a weight had been lifted from our shoulders, but everyone’s experience is different depending on what you need.

What to wear to a soundbath?

Wear very comfy clothes, it doesn’t need to be yoga wear, it can be your favorite sweatpants and hoodie or your pajamas to just slide into bed after the session.

Who needs to experience a soundbath?

- Feeling anxious and overworked
- Feeling stressed out and depressed
- Bored and need a break from routine
- Meditator who wants to dive deep into your meditation practice
- Multitasker who wants to gain clarity and insight for a project
- Children and seniors.
- Basically anyone who needs some self care.

Breathwork

What is breathwork?

Breathwork is a general term used to describe any type of therapy that utilizes breathing exercises to improve mental, physical, and spiritual health.During breathwork you intentionally change your breathing pattern.

There are many forms of breathwork therapy that involves breathing in a conscious and systematic way. Many people find breathwork promotes deep relaxation or leaves them feeling energized.

Breathwork Practices

There are several breathwork approaches. You may want to try out a few different techniques over time to see which type most resonates with you and brings about the best results.
Types of breathwork include:
● Shamanic Breathwork
● Vivation
● Transformational Breath
● Holotropic Breathwork
● Clarity Breathwork
● Rebirthing

What happens during a breathwork session?

Depending on the practice you are being guided, you use different forms of conscious breathing exercises to move the energy in your body. Breathing is a vital part of life; it helps deliver oxygen into your bloodstream and remove carbon dioxide. Completing a full breath cycle involves your whole body—your chest, belly, back, and mind. It takes effort to coordinate all elements of the breath, even though the simple process seems effortless. Breathwork can help you reach a deeper state of mind. While in this state, you may be able to access buried emotions, grudges, and traumas, and ultimately release yourself from their grip on your mental state. When releasing body and emotional traumas some people express the release through crying, screaming, laughing and everyone’s experience is different. Some tingling in your hands and feet can be felt and lobster claws, the medical term for the phenomenon is “tetany”—convulsive tension triggered, in this case, by rapid, shallow breathing, which creates a deficiency of carbon dioxide in the blood, if it happens you can control the tetany by breathing through the nose.

What happens after breathwork class?

Most clients see results instantly, a feeling that your senses were dialed all the way up and you get hit with a massive surge of creative energy.

Who needs to experience breathwork?

Breathwork is used to help to improve a wide range of issues including:
● anger issues
● anxiety
● chronic pain
● depression
● emotional effects of illness
● grief
● trauma and post-traumatic stress disorder (PTSD)
